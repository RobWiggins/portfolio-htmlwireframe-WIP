Who are you anyways? Give me the facts.

A graduate from UNC Chapel Hill’s undergraduate Kenan-Flagler business school ’16, I am transitioning from a background in business, investment management, and corporate client advisory to a career in software and web development. Having taken most of the intermediate-level programming (Java, C) and CS math courses through NC State University, I have built a fundamental base through which to expand my knowledge of software engineering. Thinkful’s demanding Full Stack web development Immersion program, of which I am currently a student, develops core front and back-end skills in JavaScript, React, jQuery, Node, SQL, HTML5, CSS3 and the server-side. I will complete the program in late September.

My story

When I began college at UNC Chapel Hill, I was under the impression that the STEM fields were reserved for the sporadic geniuses of the world to conquer. Aren’t I supposed to have a high GPA to get a job? My eventual choice of corporate finance and investment management fulfilled a certain technical requirement that my mind unconsciously gravitated towards. The flow and distribution of capital are the metaphorical infrastructure of society. Through a variety of corporate advisory and investment roles, I learned key lessons about how the world operates, developed a sharpened eye for customer needs, and gained proficiency personally serving clients on the front line.

Yet a piece of me that yearned for deeper technical challenges nagged daily. A plateau of the learning curve on the horizon is disheartening. At the pace this field is growing, I'm not sure such a thing exists. I love solving problems. I love building things. I love programming. It's amazing that I may be able to do all at once. I hope to forge these passions through the lens of a trained business eye to help my future teammates succeed.

My philosophy

Software is notoriously difficult to get right. I’ve realized that I thrive on failing often then succeeding often, doing my best to maintain evenness of mind throughout. The more we fail when pushing boundaries, the more we learn. I’m a strong believer that leaping from failure to failure with undiminished enthusiasm fosters peak performance and astonishing growth. Don’t take this too personally, but I hope to fail with you too. Then we can create something wonderful.

Some of my past work

Trivia Quiz Application

Fetching question data from the Open Trivia DB API containing over 4000+ questions, the trivia quiz application enables the user to play through a five-question quiz which immediately informs the user of correctness following every answer. After completion of the quiz, the user is prompted for a decision to play again. The application maintains a history of the user’s highest score until it is terminated.
